Web accessibility

Web accessibility means that websites, tools, and technologies are designed and developed so that people with disabilities can use them. More specifically, people can: perceive, understand, navigate, and interact with the Web.

major role in providing accessibility

  1. Screen Readers: Screen readers are software applications that interpret and read aloud the text displayed on a computer screen. They are essential for people with visual impairments.
  2. Screen Magnifiers: Screen magnifiers enlarge portions of the screen, making it easier for people with low vision to see the content.
  3. Braille Displays: Braille displays provide tactile output by converting digital text into Braille characters, allowing people who are blind or have low vision to read digital content.
  4. Alternative Keyboards and Input Devices: These include keyboards with larger keys, specialized key layouts, or alternative input methods such as sip-and-puff devices or eye-tracking systems, which are helpful for individuals with mobility impairments.
  5. Speech Recognition Software: Speech recognition software allows users to control a computer or dictate text using voice commands, benefiting individuals with mobility impairments or those who have difficulty typing.
